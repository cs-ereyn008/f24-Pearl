Violation: Speeding in a Residential Zone

Legal Text: State Law Code 46.2-878: Speed Limits in Residential Areas

(a) It shall be unlawful for any person to drive a motor vehicle on the highways of the Commonwealth at a speed in excess of twenty-five miles per hour in a business or residential district when the applicable speed limit is not otherwise indicated by signs.

(b) A violation of this section shall constitute a traffic infraction punishable by a fine of not more than $200 for a first offense and $500 for a second or subsequent offense.

Simplified Explanation:

  • Speed Limit: In residential areas, the speed limit is set to 25 mph unless signs indicate otherwise. This is to ensure the safety of residents, pedestrians, and children who may be playing in the area.
  • Penalties: If you are caught driving faster than 25 mph in a residential zone:
    • For the first offense, you may be fined up to $200.
    • For any additional offenses, the fine can go up to $500.

What You Should Do:

  • Always check for speed limit signs when driving in residential areas.
  • Maintain a speed of 25 mph or lower if no signs are present.
  • If you receive a ticket for speeding, you can choose to pay the fine or contest it in court.
